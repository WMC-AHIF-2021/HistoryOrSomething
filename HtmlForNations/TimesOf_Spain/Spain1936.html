The young Republic actually was a very modest and democratic state.
Besides regular voting rights, women also were allowed to vote. The constitution seperated the church and the state. Also, the first statutes of autonomy for Catalonia and Euskadi were approved.

The government implemented many reforms, as well. The education-law was changed, land reforms were established to distribute the land with more equality and a labour-reform was written.

Sadly, many people in Spain didn't accept these changes. Violent conflicts happened over time, leading to the begin of the Spanish civil war.
