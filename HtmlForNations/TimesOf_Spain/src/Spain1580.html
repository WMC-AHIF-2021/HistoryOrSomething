Unification of Iberia

With the death of the Portuguese king Henry came a succession crisis, having three heirs claiming the throne: Infanta Catarina, Antonio Prior of Crato and Phillipp II, King of Spain.
In 1580, Antonio was declared king by the people of Santarém, but Phillipp escaped to Spain and proclaimed him to be the legal successor of Henry. After 1 year of unclear heritage, Phillipp beat Antonio's troups and was proclaimed king of Portugal, setting the begin of the Iberian Union under the Habsburg Dynasty.

Restoration War

After Phillipp's death, his son, Phillip III, became king. However, he had a different approach to Portuguese Issues. Rising taxes mainly affected portuguese merchants. Portuguese Nobles lost much of their importance, and government posts in Portugal were occupied by Spaniards. Ultimately, Phillipp tried to make Portugal a royal province, leading to the nobles losing all of their power.

Several other problems also damaged Portuguese support of their union with Spain, ending in the Restoration War in 1640.
