Spanish Empire - 1790

Info Table
Capital Madrid
President Charles IV
Type of Government Absolute monarchy
Population 16.4 million
Land Area 13,700,000 km²
Currency Spanish real
Big but powerless

After the end of the Spanish Succession War (1701-1714), the rule of the Habsburgs in Spain ended and the House of Bourbon took over. It also marked the beginning of reform and decline for the Spanish Empire.

In 1788, Charles III died and his son Charles IV became king. His father, is considered "the most successful monarch of Spain after Ferdinand and Isabel", because of his intelligent and firm leadership. Historians say that Charles III "rescued the Spaniards after waiting for a century".

Unfortunately, his son Charles IV is not really interested in ruling, but hunting. Because of this, most of the responsibility goes to his ministers and many of his decisions are influenced by his wife, Maria Luisa of Parma.
