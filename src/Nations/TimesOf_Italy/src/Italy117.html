Ave Caesar!

The Roman Empire reached its peak with the reign of Emperor Trajan and his conquest of Dacia against the Parthians. It stretches from the most eastern point of mesopotamia to the island of Britannica in the north until the most western point of the Iberian peninsula. For centuries, it completely dominates foreign policies and actions in europe, and it still looks likely that Rome will continue to do so. During the last hundred years of conquest and war the Romans developed a strong influence on their lands and outside. Language, religion, art, architecture literature, philosophy, low and forms of government were brought into the regions they possessed.

Change of Policy

Emperor Trajan still held expansionist ambitions, but with his death in 117 and the succession of Hadrian, these policies changed. Hadrian focused on the fixation of the roman borders and their fortification. He wanted to invest into the people’s unity and stable regions in the empire. He supported many building projects and civil and religious institutions, such as the Pantheon in Rome, the Temple of Venus and Roma and the Serapeum of Alexandria.
