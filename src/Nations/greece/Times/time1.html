A small country

The 19th century was tough for Greece. It lacked natural ressources and it couldn’t make any big exports. This heavily affected the Greek economy, and despite some Greeks became rich due to being merchants but the peasantry got only to no wealth.

Luckily for Greece, the war between Russia and the Ottoman Empire from 1866 to 1869 resulted in the cession of Thessaly and parts of Epirus to Greece. Despite this, many Greeks still wanted to regain the island of Crete.

Making matters worse, Greece suffered from great instability and a weak economy. The King, George I., and his government were able to improve the situation by introducing the dedilomeni principle. However, this created new problems such as higher corruption and increased taxes.

By the 1890s, Greece was bankrupt, many emigrated to the US. However, there still were some good things. Despite heavy financial problems, Greece was able to improve intern infrastructure and public buildings and in 1896, Greece was able to succesfully revive the Olympic Games.
