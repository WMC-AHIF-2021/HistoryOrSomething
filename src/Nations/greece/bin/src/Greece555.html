The West has fallen, due to inner instability and conflicts. The eastern half, however, was much more stable and wealthier. The now called byzantine Empire, however, wouldn’t be able to reclaim its lost lands or use their full power, because they had to focus on the conflict between them and the Sassanids in the east. Both of them would eventually wear each other out.

Over time, Byzantium became less roman and more greek, which also was a reason why they were called Byzantines and not Romans. However, they still saw themselves as successors of the Roman Empire.

In 527, Justinian I. came to power. He had a dream to reestablish the Roman Empire and he nearly made it. Over just half a century he regained lost territory in Italy, norther Africa and even Iberia. The time of the Romans might have been over, but not of the Byzantines.
