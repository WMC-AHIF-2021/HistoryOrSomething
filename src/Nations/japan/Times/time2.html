Russo-Japanese War

In the last years, Russia expanded into Siberia, however, due to the cold and long winter supply by ship was difficult. So Russia was in need for warm water for trade and its growing naval force. Its czar, Nicholas I., focused on the Korean and Liadong peninsulas. The Japanese were afraid of growing russian influence in the region and they offered control over Manchuria for Japan maintaining influence over Korea. Russia refused and a few months later, they opted for war.

The war lasted for 1 year and ended in an embarrassing defeat for Russia. It had to dismiss its espansionist ambitions in asia. Japan's victory surprised many onlookers, and resulted in Japan becoming a great power and shifting the balance of power in asia, but also europe. The war also marked the first victory of an Asian country against a Western power in modern times.

Rise of Japan

Since the Meiji-Restoration, Japan rapidly industrialized, adopted Western ideas and reformed its political and social structure, going from a feudal lose nation to a modern industrialized state. And despite small setbacks, Japan managed to establish its place in the world as great power.
