Where are we?

It's the medieval period and that means knights, conquest and much aristocracy. But of course, not everything was clear written, because there was much potential of conflict. For example, borders were not set like today and only a few nations had a proper government.

France is an decentralised state, marked by the fragmentation of the Carolingian Empire, after which it became West Francia. Unfortunately its royal family, the House of Capet, was a quite weak ruling crownhouse. As a result, many areas became uncontrolled, like Normandy or Burgundy, and began to govern themselves.

At least, with the reign of Philip the Kingdom did experience a modest recovery.
