Britain's imperial Century

With Napoleon defeated and France gone from the world stage as polical rival for Britain, the Empire had no real competitiors and was now the worlds only superpower. It also seized a quite amount of territory during the wars, such as many ports in India and cape colony. This saw the beginning of a new era, which is referred as Britain‘s „imperial century“. In the next century, Britain takes the position as globle policeman (Pax Britannica) and a foreign polica named „splendid isolation“. Also, Britain controlled a huge part of the world economy. This meant they also controlled many state’s economies such as from China, Argentina and Siam. In China, this escalated in the First Opium War (1839-1842), which Britain won, gaining Hong Kong and other ports like Shanghai.
